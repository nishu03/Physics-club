Welcome to the physics club

The primary goal of the Physics Club is to incite and foster interest in the study and practice of Physics. The club hopes to promote and broaden the experience in physics and astronomy as well as in related fields. In doing so, The Physics Club lends itself to advocating interaction between the department and its students in order to expose the latter to the wonderful and exciting opportunities available through the study and practice of physics and astronomy.

We do hope you will be able to come to learn and understand different concepts of physics.

All the matter that makes up the human race could fit in a sugar cube. That’s right. Everyone you’ve ever known… everyone who’s ever lived, for that matter… all of us could be squashed into a little hunk of matter about half-an-inch on each side, if only you reduced us down to our basic components. This video reports a Physics lesson using video tracking analysis applied in Newton's Cradle experiment to train student's multiple representation skill. Newton's Cradle is commonly used to demonstrate the law of energy and momentum conservation. It consists of swinging spherical bobs which transfers energy from one to another by means of elastic collisions. From the video analysis, it is found that there is a difference in the velocity of the two bobs of opposite ends. Furthermore, investigation of what might cause it to happen can be done by observing and analysing the recorded video.
